Guidelines for Internship Program

Read through the guidelines carefully before starting your internship program.

1. Program Objectives

  • Provide students with practical experience to complement their academic learning.
  • Help students build industry-relevant skills, enhance their resumes, and expand their professional networks.
  • Encourage students to explore potential career paths, making informed decisions about their future.
  • Facilitate collaborations between educational institutions and industry partners.

2. Eligibility Criteria for Students

  • Open to students in grades 11 and 12 (or grade 10, depending on the nature of the internship).
  • Must have completed relevant courses on CareerCraft that align with the internship's field.
  • Must submit an internship application that includes: Name, Grade, Contact information, Courses completed and badges earned, and a statement of interest explaining why they want to join the internship.

3. Eligibility Criteria for Partner Companies

  • Industry partners must have a proven record of providing quality training and mentorship.
  • Commit to provide a structured internship plan with clear learning outcomes.
  • Commit to a minimum of 4 weeks of internship duration, with flexible schedules.
  • Provide necessary resources, tools, and guidance to interns.
  • Assign a dedicated mentor/supervisor to oversee the intern's progress and provide feedback.

4. Internship Matching & Application Process

Internship Listings: Companies can post internship opportunities on the CareerCraft platform, detailing the duration and description.

Student Applications: Students can browse internships and apply through the portal by submitting their resume, and statement of purpose.

Matching Algorithm: The system will use AI algorithms to match students to internships based on their skills, badges, and interests.

Selection Process: Companies can review applications and conduct interviews or assessments if needed.

5. Internship Duration & Structure

  • Internships can range from 4 to 12 weeks depending on the nature of the project.
  • Can be part-time (10-20 hours per week) or full-time (30-40 hours per week).
  • Flexible formats include remote, on-site, or hybrid internships.

Each internship should have:

  • Clear Learning Objectives: Defined goals that students should achieve by the end of the program.
  • Structured Timeline: Specific tasks or projects assigned to students, with regular check-ins.
  • Mid-term and Final Evaluations: To assess the student's performance and progress.

6. Evaluation & Feedback System

Student Self-Assessment:

At the midpoint and end of the internship, students complete a self-evaluation form to reflect on their experience.

Mentor Evaluation:

  • Mentors provide feedback on the intern's performance, skills demonstrated, and areas for improvement.
  • A scoring rubric can be used to rate: Communication, teamwork, Ability to take initiative and solve problems, Adaptability and willingness to learn.

7. Code of Conduct for Interns

  • Professionalism: Interns are expected to behave professionally, meet deadlines, and maintain a positive attitude.
  • Confidentiality: Interns must not disclose any confidential information obtained during the internship.
  • Punctuality & Attendance: Interns must adhere to the agreed-upon schedule and notify their supervisor in case of absences.

8. Benefits for Interns

  • Real-World Experience: Work on live projects that align with industry standards.
  • Mentorship: Direct guidance from industry experts to enhance skills and gain insights into career paths.
  • Networking Opportunities: Build connections with professionals that could lead to future job offers.
  • Skill Badges & Certificates: Earn recognition on CareerCraft for completing internships.

9. Incentives for Partner Companies

  • Access to Highly Motivated Students: Partner with highly motivated students who have demonstrated proficiency through badges.
  • Early Recruitment Option: The option to hire interns as full-time employees after graduation.
  • Brand Recognition: Partner companies are featured on CareerCraft, enhancing their reputation among the student community.

10. Tools & Resources Provided

  • Internship Dashboard: A centralized platform where students can track their progress, communicate with mentors, and submit assignments.
  • Resource Library: Access to guides, case studies, and best practice resources for successful internships.
  • Chat System: A dedicated chat feature for interns to interact with their mentors and peers.

11. Safety & Compliance

  • Ensure all internships comply with local labor laws and student protection policies.
  • Provide a mechanism for students to report any issues or concerns during their internship.
